Pascal Rüegger - Musician, Dancer, Developer, Broadcaster

Jazz, Folk, Classical, and Especially Everything in Between


I love music. I love a lot of different styles of music. And I love a lot of different instruments. Starting with classical violin at age six and having done a Master's degree in jazz bass on the way I have played more than a dozen different instruments in a wide range of groups, from a symphonic orchestra via freejazz projects to traditional dance music. Currently I am focusing on mandolin, irish flute, guitar and double bass, playing mostly music that's somewhere in between jazz and folk. Somewhere along the way I also picked up soundengineering, doing live and studio recordings and mixing.

As a member of several band projects I play public concerts aswell as music for private events. Whether you are looking for furious dancing or a smooth background - I am very happy to create the music that fits your event best.


Have a look at some of my current projects:


Rüegger Schraff Duo

A duo formed by two multi-instrumentalists who enjoy crossing musical borders. Inspirations are taken in Scandinavian and celtic traditional music aswell as in contemporary jazz, combining dancing tunes with a playfull mindset full of improvisation.

KNEP

Knep is a duo based on scandinavian music and silly hairy stuff. It's a guitar, an irish flute, a fiddle and a nyckelharpa. It's a debate about octopi on stage. It's a solution for how to wear a hat. It's a revolutionary way of surviving in cold conditions. Find out more!

Rüegger Kirchhofer Guggenheim

The Trio of Andrea Kirchhofer (fiddle), Jonas Guggenheim (accordion) and Pascal Rüegger (guitar, dance instruction) plays traditional music from all across europe for private events and parties, if desired with ad hoc dance instruction for everyone.

Balfolk, Scandinavian, Improvisation


I have been working with traditional dances and contact improvisation for many years, including one year of full-time studies in traditional scandinavian dances at Eric Sahlström Institutet in Tobo, Sweden.

I have been teaching balfok, scandinavian dances and contact improvisation in different places and settings across Europe and am always looking to keep broadening my horizon when it comes to dance and movement.
